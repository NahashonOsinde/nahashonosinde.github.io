PID control based on a Siemens S7-1200 PLC

The objective of this project was to monitor and control water levels using the PID (Proportional, Integral, Derivative) method, implemented on a Siemens S7-1200 PLC. Digital twin based on TIA Portal and Siemens NX

  • Institute: Siemens Mechatronic Training Center
  • Date: 01 Jan 2019 - 15 Aug 2019
  • Article URL: Article

Process Modelling of Geothermal Drilling System Using Digital Twin for Real-Time Monitoring and Control

Kenya predominantly relies on hydroelectric power, which is unreliable due to fluctuating rainfall patterns. Geothermal energy is proposed as a cleaner and more dependable alternative to meet the country's growing energy demands. However, geothermal drilling operations are often disrupted by tool wear and breakages, significantly increasing operational costs. This project presents a digital twin model of a geothermal drilling tool head, designed and implemented as part of a mechatronic assembly system. Using Siemens NX, TIA Portal, and an S7-1200 PLC, the model replicates the physical system's operations via OPC (open platform communications) standard. The results demonstrated that a digital twin can effectively mimic real-time operations, offering a solution for better monitoring and cost efficiency in geothermal drilling.

PhD thesis

  • University: Universitè Bourgogne Franche Comtè
  • Research institute: Femto-ST
  • Date: 01 Oct 2020 - 22 March 2024
  • Thesis URL: Thesis

Adaptive scanning and 3D motion artefact compensation in optical coherence tomography.

This research focuses on enhancing Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT), a key imaging technique for high-resolution visualisation of biological tissues, widely used in oncology for early cancer detection and optical biopsy. The study addresses challenges such as the trade-off between acquisition frequency and spatial resolution, as well as geometric artefacts caused by mechanical scanning. The key contributions include: (i) the use of non-raster trajectories for faster and efficient OCT data acquisition, (ii) an adaptive strategy to optimise data collection by targeting informative regions, and (iii) a methodology for estimating motion and shape from distorted OCT volumes. These innovations aim to improve OCT speed, accuracy, and overall efficiency.

Calibration of a multi-mobile coil magnetic manipulation system utilizing a control-oriented magnetic model

In this project, we addressed the calibration of a family of magnetic manipulation systems composed of several coils that are moved around by serial robot manipulators. We showed that the calibration of the whole system ultimately results in calibrating the manipulator and coil separately up to an unknown rigid transformation. For calibration of the coil, we proposed to use a model that has not been used so far in the literature; a control-oriented model which is sufficiently accurate and computes the magnetic field in real time. A protocol for calibrating the magnetic manipulation system using the Nelder-Mead algorithm to estimate the model parameters is presented. Calibration was performed through simulations and validated experimentally on a physical system. It was observed that the root mean square error was reduced by 37% after calibration of the physical system, indicating an improvement in accurately estimating the magnetic model.

4D printing and characterization of shape memory polymer (SMP) based smart gripper

Shape Memory Polymers (SMPs) are smart materials capable of returning to their original shape when triggered by stimuli like heat, light, or electricity. This project explored the potential of SMPs for 4D printing self-recoverable structures, such as a reconfigurable gripper designed for applications in medicine and robotics. The gripper design was 3D printed with varying parameters, including temperature, infill density, and speed, using an Ultimaker 2 Printer. Results showed that optimal print quality and shape recovery were achieved with a printing temperature of 203 °C, an infill density of 38%, and a speed of 40 mm/s. Analysis revealed that lower infill densities resulted in higher recovery rates due to reduced stiffness, with the best recovery rate reaching 96.93%. These findings demonstrate the potential of SMPs in creating efficient, self-recoverable structures for advanced applications.

Amor fati

  • Category: Stoic philosophy
  • URL: Daily stoic

Love your fate

Amor fati is a Latin phrase that may be translated as "love of fate" or "love of one's fate". It is used to describe an attitude in which one sees everything that happens in one's life, including suffering and loss, as good or, at the very least, necessary. Amor fati is often associated with what Friedrich Nietzsche called "eternal recurrence", the idea that everything recurs infinitely over an infinite period of time. From this he developed a desire to be willing to live exactly the same life over and over for all eternity
